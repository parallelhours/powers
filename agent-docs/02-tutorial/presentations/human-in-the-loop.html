What Is It?

The Human-in-the-Loop Cadence

Most AI-assisted development falls into one of two traps. The HITL cadence avoids both by defining clear handoff points where human judgment is required and AI autonomy is bounded.

Micromanage Human controls every keystroke — defeats the purpose of AI assistance. Slow, exhausting, and doesn't scale.
Step Away Human disappears and the agent drifts. Ambiguity compounds, requirements diverge, bugs surface weeks later.
Solution: A structured, recursive process where every step is guided by a human and executed with AI assistance. The human approves outputs, makes decisions, and sets direction. The AI executes, drafts, and accelerates.

Why It Matters

Catch Divergence from Goals Early

When an agent runs unchecked, ambiguity in requirements compounds: a vague PRD phrase → misunderstood architecture decision → incorrectly implemented feature → bug weeks later.

HITL takes more time per step but lets the human course correct early and often — filling gaps, flagging misunderstandings, adjusting direction.

Core Principle

Shrink to Fit

The same process applies at every scale. Phases collapse or compress based on context. The invariant: human approval at each boundary.

Foundations

Phases 0–2: Setup & Requirements

Before the cadence runs, the project needs its foundation. Then requirements and architecture follow.

Phase 0 Project Setup Diataxis documentation structure, deep dives on codebase, persona definitions Architect + Developer
Phase 1 Requirements PRD with context, functional/non-functional requirements, prioritization, success criteria Product Marketing
Phase 2 Architecture System architecture, technology decisions, interfaces, Architecture Decision Records Architect
Each phase delivers an approved artifact before the next begins. The human reviews and approves every output.

Planning

Phase 3: Sprint Breakdown

Developer and Project Manager personas turn requirements and architecture into an actionable plan.

1. Task Breakdown Decompose work into discrete, estimatable tasks using the story template
2. Dual Estimates Each task gets two estimates: Human hours (without AI) and AI-assisted hours
3. Sprint Definition Group tasks into sprints with clear goals. Document flow, milestones, and risk areas.
Review the estimates — debate any AI estimate that seems too aggressive or too conservative. Approve sprint structure before implementation begins.

Execution

The Implementation Loop

Every task follows the same pattern. The review step is AI-augmented.

AI-Augmented Code Review Humans may use AI from a different LLM provider for an independent second opinion. Initially the AI acts as an enhanced reviewer — flagging issues for human evaluation. As trust builds, delegation widens: first routine changes, then broader reviews, eventually AI-conducted approvals. Code review guidelines must be written into the agent's instructions (AGENTS.md, CLAUDE.md) covering correctness, test coverage, style, security, performance, and documentation.

Review & Close

Sprint, Milestone & Project Reviews

Sprint Review What was completed vs. planned, what wasn't and why, time tracked (human vs. AI), blockers, updated estimates for remaining work
Milestone Review Working software demo, human vs. AI time across all sprints, estimate accuracy, velocity trend, retro, updated roadmap
Project Completion Final delivery of all milestones, project retro, knowledge base updates, close-out metrics (total time, velocity, estimate accuracy)
Milestone reviews are key trust-building moments. Consistent deliveries justify wider delegation in future phases.
